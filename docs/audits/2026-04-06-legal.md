**Auditor role:** Head of Legal & Compliance (canonical role defined in `~/.claude/audits/legal.md`)
**Project:** Voyager (formerly "Agentic Travel Agent")
**Production domain:** `https://interviewiangreenough.xyz`
**Overall posture:** **Missing**

---

## Executive Summary

Voyager is a public-facing, AI-powered travel planning product that:

1. Accepts user registration and stores personal data (email, name, conversations, trip history) in a Postgres database on Neon.
2. Sends user messages and trip parameters to Anthropic's Claude API.
3. Sends trip parameters to SerpApi (Google Flights, Google Hotels) and to Google Places.
4. Is reachable from any jurisdiction, including the EU (GDPR) and California (CCPA / CPRA).
5. Sets at least one persistent session cookie (`cookie-parser` + DB-backed session) and loads Vercel Analytics and Vercel Speed Insights.

Voyager has **no Terms of Service, no Privacy Policy, no Cookie Policy, no Acceptable Use Policy, no AI disclosure document, no DPA trail, and no data subject rights mechanism**. The registration page (`web-client/src/app/(auth)/register/page.tsx:155-159`) asks users to agree to a "Terms of Service" and "Privacy Policy" that do not exist. Both links point to `/faq`. The FAQ page contains statements of fact about data handling that are **factually incorrect** relative to what the code actually does (it names Amadeus as the data source when the server uses SerpApi). This is the highest-risk finding in the audit, because it is both a missing-document problem AND an active misrepresentation problem.

### Top 3 launch blockers

1. **No Privacy Policy exists, yet the registration flow claims the user has agreed to one.** Pointing `/faq` links at a non-existent Privacy Policy is a direct misrepresentation to users at the moment of consent. This alone is enough to draw an FTC Section 5 "deceptive practice" theory in the US and to trigger Article 13 / Article 14 GDPR violations in the EU. P0, launch blocker.
2. **The FAQ (which is the de facto privacy disclosure today) describes the wrong data processors.** It says flights and hotels come from "the Amadeus API, the same data source used by major travel agencies worldwide." The server actually calls SerpApi (which in turn scrapes Google Flights / Google Hotels). Users are being told their queries go to one company when they actually go to a different one. P0.
3. **No Terms of Service exists.** Without a TOS, Voyager has no limitation of liability, no disclaimer of warranties, no binding arbitration clause, no governing-law clause, no acceptable use policy, and no right to terminate abusive users. For an AI product that generates travel recommendations (a domain where users can and do sue over inaccurate pricing and missed flights), this is an unacceptable exposure. P0, launch blocker.

## Privacy & Data Protection

**Data collected (based on code review):**

- Email, password (hashed), name (registration)
- Session tokens (cookie-based, DB-stored hash)
- Full conversation history with the AI agent (trip parameters, free-text user messages)
- Saved trips and itineraries
- User preferences (`server/src/handlers/userPreferences`)
- API cache entries (`server/migrations/1771879388549_create-api-cache-table.js`)
- IP address (rate limiter middleware)
- Analytics telemetry via Vercel Analytics and Vercel Speed Insights

**Data shared with third parties (by the actual code, not by the FAQ's description):**

- **Anthropic**: full user messages and conversation state, every turn
- **SerpApi**: trip queries including destinations, dates, and origin airports
- **Google Places API**: location search strings
- **Supabase**: if used for auth (FULL_APPLICATION_SPEC references it; actual auth appears to be custom Express sessions. Confirm which)
- **Neon**: all DB data at rest
- **Railway**: all server traffic, Redis cache
- **Vercel**: frontend delivery, Analytics, Speed Insights

**Findings:**

- **P0.** The FAQ (`web-client/src/app/faq/page.tsx:38-41`) says the data source is Amadeus. The codebase (`server/src/services/serpapi.service.ts`, `server/src/tools/flights.tool.ts`, `server/src/tools/hotels.tool.ts`) uses SerpApi. This is a material misrepresentation to users about who receives their data. Must be fixed before the next deploy regardless of whether a formal Privacy Policy is in place.
- **P0.** FAQ says "Your data is never sold." This claim is not verifiable without a Privacy Policy and a formal data-sale policy. Under CPRA, "sale" is defined broadly enough that sharing for cross-context behavioral advertising counts. Vercel Analytics processing must be evaluated against this definition.
- **P0.** No data subject rights mechanism. FAQ promises deletion "within 30 days" (`faq/page.tsx:88-91`) but there is no evidence the deletion actually cascades through the trip history, conversation log, cached API responses, Anthropic's retention window, or SerpApi's logs. A promise Voyager cannot operationally fulfill is worse than no promise.
- **P1.** No mention of data retention periods, no mention of legal basis for processing (GDPR Art. 6), no mention of international data transfers (Anthropic and Google are US-based; EU users need Standard Contractual Clauses).
- **P1.** IP addresses are logged by the rate limiter. This is a personal identifier under GDPR. Needs documentation.

## Cookie Consent & Tracking

**Cookies / tracking technologies observed:**

- Session cookie (essential, DB-backed). `server/src/app.ts:59`, `server/src/constants/session.ts`. Essential and therefore consent-exempt under ePrivacy, but must still be disclosed.
- Vercel Analytics. `web-client/src/app/layout.tsx:41`. Vercel Analytics can run in cookieless mode, but whether Voyager has configured it that way is not visible in the code. Assume non-compliant until proven otherwise.
- Vercel Speed Insights. `web-client/src/app/layout.tsx:42`. Similar situation.

**Findings:**

- **P1.** No cookie consent banner anywhere in the layout. For any non-essential cookie set before consent, the EU position is that it is a violation regardless of how "harmless" the cookie is.
- **P2.** Even if Vercel Analytics is cookieless, the processing of IP addresses for analytics purposes requires a lawful basis under GDPR and must be disclosed.

## Terms of Service & Acceptable Use

**Current state:** Nothing exists. The register page links "Terms of Service" and "Privacy Policy" to `/faq`, which is not a TOS or a Privacy Policy.

**Findings:**

- **P0.** No TOS. No limitation of liability. No disclaimer that travel information may be inaccurate, outdated, or unsuitable. An AI travel product that hallucinates a flight price and causes a user to miss a business trip has real exposure.
- **P0.** No binding arbitration clause, no class action waiver, no governing law clause. Voyager is defaulted into whatever forum a plaintiff chooses.
- **P1.** No AUP. Users could use the agent to plan travel that violates sanctions (OFAC concerns for travel to certain countries) or to generate recommendations for sanctioned entities. Voyager has no contractual grounds to terminate such a user today.
- **P1.** No age gating. Need a "13+" or "16+" minimum (COPPA / GDPR age-of-consent). Register flow does not ask for age.

## Refund & Cancellation

The FAQ claims a "$9/month Pro plan" with "unlimited trips" (`faq/page.tsx:60-63`). No payment integration is visible in the codebase. This is aspirational copy presented as fact.

**Findings:**

- **P1.** Advertising a product that does not exist, at a specific price, with specific features, is a textbook FTC deceptive practice. Either build the paid plan or remove the pricing language.
- **P1.** If and when the paid plan ships, a Refund Policy and a Click-to-Cancel flow are required.

## AI-Specific Disclosures

**EU AI Act (Art. 52. Transparency obligations for AI systems interacting with natural persons):** Voyager is required to clearly disclose to EU users that they are interacting with an AI system. The current chat UI does not include this disclosure prominently. The FAQ mentions it, but the FAQ is not where users start.

**California AB 2013:** Requires disclosure of the training data sources of generative AI. This duty falls on the model provider (Anthropic), not on Voyager, but Voyager should reference Anthropic's disclosures.

**Findings:**

- **P0.** Add an explicit "You are chatting with an AI. Output may be inaccurate." disclosure in the chat UI at the point of first interaction, not buried in the FAQ.
- **P1.** Add a model-accuracy disclaimer. The FAQ currently says the agent "won't fabricate data" (`faq/page.tsx:111-113`). LLMs can and do fabricate data; this claim cannot be substantiated.
- **P1.** The hero and landing copy says "No hallucinated prices. Every recommendation comes from live API data." (`page.tsx:206`). This is a strong factual claim about model behavior. Voyager cannot prove the agent will never paraphrase a price incorrectly. Soften to "Recommendations are grounded in live API data" or similar.

## Prioritized Recommendations

### Launch blockers (P0. Fix before the next public deploy)

1. **Remove the dead Terms of Service and Privacy Policy links from the register page** until real documents exist, or publish stub `/terms` and `/privacy` pages (even if they say "coming soon, contact legal@..."). Claiming agreement to non-existent documents is worse than having no disclosure at all. (`web-client/src/app/(auth)/register/page.tsx:155-159`)
2. **Correct the FAQ's false statement about the data source.** Replace "Amadeus API" with "SerpApi, which aggregates from Google Flights and Google Hotels." (`web-client/src/app/faq/page.tsx:38-41`)
3. **Remove the $9/month Pro plan claim** from the FAQ, or ship it. Do not advertise a non-existent paid product. (`faq/page.tsx:60-63`)
4. **Remove the "No hallucinated prices" claim** from the landing page. Soften to "Recommendations grounded in live API data." (`page.tsx:206`)
5. **Draft and publish a Privacy Policy.** Name every processor (Anthropic, SerpApi, Google Places, Supabase, Neon, Railway, Vercel), describe what data is sent to each, and link from the footer and from the register page. Include a DSAR contact address.
6. **Draft and publish a Terms of Service.** Include: disclaimer of warranties, limitation of liability, governing law, dispute resolution, acceptable use, termination. Link from the footer and from the register page.
7. **Add an AI disclosure banner** to the chat UI: "You are chatting with an AI agent. Its responses may be incorrect. Verify flight and hotel prices before booking."

### Pre-launch (P1)

8. **Sign DPAs** with Anthropic, Supabase, Neon, Vercel, Railway, and Google Cloud. Investigate whether SerpApi offers one. Save copies.
9. **Add a cookie consent banner** for EU/UK visitors, or confirm Vercel Analytics is cookieless and document that decision.
10. **Build a real account deletion endpoint** that cascades across `trips`, `conversations`, `user_preferences`, `api_cache`, and `tool_call_log`. Add an export endpoint.
11. **Add an accessibility statement** at `/accessibility`.
12. **Run a USPTO TESS search** for "Voyager" in travel and SaaS classes. If conflicts exist, escalate to outside counsel.
13. **Add a "sub-processors" page** listing every third party.
14. **Soften marketing claims** that cannot be substantiated.
15. **Add age gating** to registration (13+ or 16+ depending on jurisdiction).

### Post-launch (P2)

16. Add an `ATTRIBUTIONS.md` for images, fonts, icons.
17. Add a license scan to CI.
18. Draft an internal RoPA (GDPR Art. 30).
19. Publish a security.txt and a responsible disclosure policy.
